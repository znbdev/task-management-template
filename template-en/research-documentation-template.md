## 💡 Research Task Usage Guide

### Information Collection Techniques
1. **Multi-channel Validation**: Cross-validate the same information from at least 2-3 independent sources
2. **Primary Sources First**: Prioritize official documents and raw data, then secondary analysis
3. **Complete Citation Records**: Save URLs, authors, and publication dates for all materials for traceability
4. **Organize While Collecting**: Don't wait until the end; spend 15 minutes daily organizing materials collected that day

### Avoiding Common Pitfalls
*   ❌ **Confirmation Bias**: Only seeking information that supports your viewpoint
    *   ✅ *Countermeasure*: Actively seek opposing viewpoints and questioning voices
*   ❌ **Information Overload**: Collecting too much useless information, wasting time
    *   ✅ *Countermeasure*: Set "stop collecting" criteria in advance, focus on core questions
*   ❌ **Superficial Analysis**: Staying at surface phenomena, lacking deep insights
    *   ✅ *Countermeasure*: Ask "why" repeatedly, dig into underlying reasons and logic
*   ❌ **Ignoring Context**: Applying mechanically without considering specific scenarios
    *   ✅ *Countermeasure*: Pay attention to applicability conditions and background constraints

### Efficient Organization Methods
*   **MECE Principle**: Ensure classifications are Mutually Exclusive and Collectively Exhaustive
*   **Pyramid Principle**: Conclusion first, top-down approach, grouped categorization, logical progression
*   **Visualization Tools**: Use mind maps, comparison tables, timelines, and other tools to present complex information

### Focus Areas for Different Research Types
*   **Market Research**: Focus on market size, growth trends, competitive landscape, user needs
*   **Competitive Analysis**: Focus on feature comparison, pricing strategy, user experience, market share
*   **Technical Research**: Focus on technology maturity, community activity, performance metrics, learning curve
*   **User Research**: Focus on user personas, pain points, behavior patterns, satisfaction
*   **Documentation**: Focus on information completeness, structural clarity, update timeliness, search convenience
